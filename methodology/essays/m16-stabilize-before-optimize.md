## 1. Why this doc exists

The first 90 days after arrival is the window where capable, organized people most reliably break themselves. The pattern is consistent across the kinds of clients Hekswerk serves: the move itself was so absorbing, the planning so structured, the pre-arrival work so thorough that arrival lands as *the moment to finally do everything*. The first week becomes a sprint of registration appointments and apartment viewings and bank visits and bureaucratic phone calls — most of it on a body that just crossed an ocean, in a city the client doesn't know yet, in a language they don't fully command.

D-07's job is to prevent that. The principle that orders D-07 is *stabilize before optimize*: the landing window first secures the systems that make life administratively, physically, and medically workable, and only then turns to refinement. Optimization without stable basics produces fragility and exhaustion — and in the Hekswerk audience specifically, the fragility shows up as the crash pattern many clients explicitly engaged the practice to prevent.

This note codifies what counts as stabilization vs. optimization, names why competent clients are the most vulnerable to optimizing too early, and specifies the D-07 ordering rules. It pairs with [[m17-arrival-cliff]] when that note exists — M-16 names the *principle*, M-17 names the *failure mode that violates it*.

Reading time: ~10 minutes end-to-end. Reference time once internalized: under 20 seconds per drafted D-07 phase decision.

## 2. Working definition

> *Stabilize before optimize* is the methodology's ordering principle for the first 90 days post-arrival: secure the systems that make life administratively, physically, and medically workable before refining any of them. *Stabilization* is presence and access — you have a place to sleep, a working ID, a way to be paid, a way to get medication, a way to communicate. *Optimization* is refinement — a better apartment, a specialist consultation, an optimized financial structure, a deeper community, a tighter routine. The methodology delivers stabilization in roughly the first 30 days and lets optimization unfold across days 30–90 and beyond.

The principle is *not* "do the easy things first." Stabilization is sometimes the harder work (registration, healthcare enrollment, banking access can each be procedurally heavy). The principle is "do the *load-bearing* things first" — the things downstream systems depend on, the things that absorb the cost of skipping later, the things whose absence makes the move feel like it's not actually happening even after the plane has landed.

The principle also is *not* a rejection of optimization. Optimization is the point of moving in the first place; nobody relocates internationally to settle for the minimum-viable version of every system indefinitely. The discipline is *timing* — optimization belongs after stabilization, on a foundation that can bear it.

## 3. The stabilization stack

The systems that constitute stabilization, in priority order. Each is *load-bearing* on the others below it; skipping or compressing earlier items creates downstream friction that's expensive to recover from.

### 1. Housing / address (registration-eligible)

A confirmed place to sleep that *also* satisfies the destination's registration requirements. Not necessarily permanent housing; not necessarily ideal housing; *registration-eligible* housing. Without this, the registration cascade (item 2) cannot begin, and without registration most downstream systems are gated.

*Stabilization-level*: temporary housing booked, confirmed registration-eligible per [[m13-verify-with-authority]], physically accessible on arrival day.

*Optimization that waits*: permanent housing search; neighborhood-fit refinement; long-term lease negotiation; furnishing beyond functional.

### 2. Municipal registration / local identifier

In the Netherlands, this is the Anmeldung-equivalent BSN. In Germany, the Anmeldung itself. In other destinations, the analogous process. This is the gate that unlocks banking, healthcare enrollment, and most government-facing systems.

*Stabilization-level*: registered at the local municipal office; identifier in hand or scheduled with a confirmed appointment date inside the legal window.

*Optimization that waits*: post-registration administrative refinement; address changes beyond initial registration.

### 3. Healthcare enrollment + medication continuity

Insurance coverage active or in clear bridge mode; GP registered or scheduled within first 30 days; any prescription medications confirmed available and bridged across the transition.

*Stabilization-level*: insurance active (gesetzlich/privat decision made if applicable; basis enrollment if not); GP registration submitted; medication bridge in place; emergency-care basics known.

*Optimization that waits*: specialist consultations beyond urgent; therapy/mental health referrals beyond bridge continuity; preventive care scheduling; insurance optimization between options.

### 4. Banking access

A local account opened and operational; payment instrument (debit card or local equivalent) in hand; some basic income/payment flow established (direct deposit if applicable; ability to receive and send local payments).

*Stabilization-level*: account opened, card received, app/online banking functional, basic payment flow tested.

*Optimization that waits*: investment account setup; cross-border financial planning beyond access; account-type optimization between options; financial structure refinement.

### 5. Phone / internet / utilities

Local SIM or phone plan; home internet active (or temporary alternative working); utility accounts set up at the temporary or permanent address; two-factor authentication continuity through the SIM transition.

*Stabilization-level*: reachable on a local number, online at home, mail receivable at the registered address.

*Optimization that waits*: plan optimization; provider switching; home office equipment beyond functional.

### 6. Capacity protection

Rest windows protected; routine established at minimum-viable level; support-structure continuity preserved (therapy continuity per [[m12-privacy-posture]] privacy-respecting handoffs; household co-regulation if applicable; reliable contact with home-side friends).

*Stabilization-level*: ≥2 recovery days per week protected; basic sleep/eat/move routine functional; support-structure connections maintained on the working schedule the client had pre-move.

*Optimization that waits*: community-building beyond initial contacts; new routines beyond minimum-viable; deep social network rebuild.

## 4. The optimization pile

The systems and refinements that are explicitly *not* stabilization, and that belong in the days 30–90 window or later. Listed not because they don't matter — they matter very much, often more than the stabilization items in the long run — but because their attempt during the stabilization window violates the principle.

- **Permanent housing search beyond minimum viable.** Apartments evaluated, viewings booked, neighborhood comparisons drawn. The single most common stabilization-window mistake.
- **Specialist healthcare beyond basic enrollment.** GP, dentist, optometrist, therapist re-establishment beyond bridge continuity.
- **Financial optimization beyond access.** Investment account opening; tax-strategy refinement; multi-currency optimization; insurance optimization.
- **Community-building beyond initial contacts.** Joining clubs, networking events, intentional friendship-building, identity-specific community engagement.
- **Language acquisition beyond functional.** Formal classes; immersion programs; certification pursuits.
- **Professional network rebuild.** Industry contacts, alumni networks, professional associations beyond what the work requires.
- **Routine refinement.** Optimizing sleep, exercise, productivity routines beyond the minimum-viable version that's keeping the body functional.
- **Cultural deep-dives.** Travel within the new country, sustained immersion in destination culture, intentional integration projects.

All of these are good. None of them belong in the first 30 days.

## 5. Why competent clients are the most vulnerable

The methodology's specific concern: the kind of clients Hekswerk serves are *unusually* vulnerable to optimizing too early. Three reasons.

### 1. The competence pattern

Clients who can plan a complex international move themselves are clients who can plan their first month abroad themselves. The competence is real. It's also exactly the trait that produces a 60-item first-week checklist with an optimized apartment search nested inside the registration cascade. The plan looks ambitious because the planner is capable; the plan crashes because no amount of capability changes the fact that there are only seven days in the first week.

The methodology's job is not to question the competence — it's earned. The methodology's job is to redirect the competence toward the things that *can* be done in week one (the stabilization stack), and to make the optimization items *visibly deferred* so the client doesn't experience the deferral as failure to plan thoroughly.

### 2. The forward-motion reflex

For many clients in the target audience, sustained planning toward the move has been a way of holding the move's emotional weight. The planning itself becomes load-bearing. Arrival removes the planning's object — the move is done, the next thing is *living there* — and the forward-motion reflex looks for new ground.

Optimization items provide that ground. There's always more to optimize. Diving into permanent housing search, joining the queer community group, signing up for German classes, scheduling the specialist — each is *forward motion*, which feels like the right shape for the energy the planning had been holding.

The methodology's job is to name this and offer different ground. The forward motion in the first 30 days is the stabilization stack; it's also rest, presence, walking the neighborhood, noticing what's around. Slower forward motion, by design.

### 3. The crash pattern many clients have explicitly named

[[m15-capacity-as-a-non-pathologizing-risk-category]] §5 describes the disclosed capacity patterns clients in the Hekswerk target audience bring to engagement — under-ask-then-crash, sustained-then-collapse, pre-burnout-handling-then-medical-leave. These patterns surface in capacity signals at intake; they appear as risk rows in D-06 (per [[m14-risk-theater-versus-risk-register]]). And they are *most likely to fire* in the first 30 days post-arrival, when:

- The planning load drops abruptly.
- The execution load arrives all at once.
- The support structure is geographically discontinuous.
- The sensory and cognitive load of new environment is sustained.
- The recovery resources (familiar food, sleep environment, language ease) are absent or reduced.

Stabilize-before-optimize is, in part, *crash-pattern prevention design*. The principle's strictness reflects the strictness of the pattern it's preventing.

## 6. D-07 ordering rules

Five concrete rules for D-07 phase sequencing. Each rule is a defensible default; each can be deviated from for specific client circumstance, but the deviation should be documented in working notes.

### Rule 1 — Week 1 is stabilization-only

The arrival week's plan contains only stabilization-stack items (§3) and recovery. No optimization-pile items (§4) appear in week 1, regardless of how attractive they seem. This includes apartment viewings, specialist appointments, community events, language classes, professional networking.

Deviation: a single time-critical item from the optimization pile may appear if its window expires within week 1 and missing it has cascading consequences (e.g., a school enrollment deadline that can't be delayed). The deviation must be flagged in D-07 prose; the rest of week 1 compresses to absorb the load.

### Rule 2 — Days 8–30 are registration, healthcare, banking, communications

The core administrative stabilization happens in this window. Anmeldung-equivalent registration completed; healthcare enrollment confirmed; banking account opened; phone/internet operational; capacity protections in place. By day 30, the client has the load-bearing systems that downstream optimization will refine.

Deviation: if any stabilization item is structurally delayed by external factors (appointment availability, regulatory processing time), the deviation is explicit and a bridge is designed (continued temporary housing; bridge insurance; alternative payment route). The deviation does not *defer* the stabilization item — it *bridges* until the item lands.

### Rule 3 — Days 31–60 are deeper setup and provider follow-through

The window where optimization pile items begin to enter — *selectively*. Specialist healthcare consultations scheduled; permanent housing search begun (or actively deferred if the stabilization-house works); professional network outreach started; routine refinement attempted. The rule: optimization items enter the plan *with explicit stabilization-check first* — is the stabilization stack actually stable, or is an item still wobbly?

Deviation: if any stabilization-stack item is still unresolved at day 31, the optimization items for that window defer. Stability is the prerequisite, not the parallel track.

### Rule 4 — Days 61–90 are consolidation and review

The window where the client reviews what stabilized, what's still wobbly, what the next 6–12 months should hold. Permanent housing decided or actively delayed with rationale; provider relationships consolidated or rerouted; routines refined into their sustainable shape; community-building begins to compound. The 90-day mark is a planned review point — *not* the moment everything has to be optimized.

Deviation: clients may legitimately want day-90 to be the moment of full operational arrival. The deviation is to compress days 61–90 — but only after acknowledging that compression here is an explicit deviation from M-16, and that the client has the capacity to absorb it.

### Rule 5 — Rest is structural, not earned

Recovery windows are pre-allocated (per [[m07-capacity-as-critical-strength-sequencing-constraint]] §4 move 5) across the full 90 days, with extra weight in days 1–14. The rule: recovery does not get *earned* by completing the day's items; it is *protected* regardless of completion. The plan that asks the client to skip rest until the to-do list is empty is the plan that triggers the crash pattern in §5.

Deviation: none. This rule is firm because the failure mode is severe and recovery is not recoverable from after the fact.

## 7. The capacity overlay in D-07

D-07 inherits the capacity sequencing discipline from [[m07-capacity-as-critical-strength-sequencing-constraint]]. Each of the four phases (week 1; days 8–30; days 31–60; days 61–90) has a capacity profile that should be visible in the rendered D-07.

The phases have *different* capacity dynamics:

- **Week 1**: lowest capacity (jetlag, sensory overload, displacement); highest recovery weight.
- **Days 8–30**: capacity rising but still constrained; administrative load also rising; tight density management required.
- **Days 31–60**: capacity stabilizing; optimization items entering selectively; density can broaden.
- **Days 61–90**: capacity near baseline (for the new baseline); consolidation work; review-shaped rather than execution-shaped.

The D-07 view should surface this profile alongside the phase content. The renderer's job is to make the capacity-stabilization-optimization interaction visible — the same density indicator from M-07 §7 applies, scaled to the 90-day window.

## 8. Worked examples

### L.B. dogfood case (US → NL, DAFT)

**Temporary housing first; permanent search deferred**

Pre-arrival plan secures registration-eligible short-term housing in Den Haag for the first 6–8 weeks. Permanent housing search does not begin until Anmeldung is complete and the household has lived a real week in the city — *not* via Zoom from Portland three months earlier. The deferral is explicit in D-07 and explicit in [[m08-reusable-circular-dependencies]] (the dual-address pattern is the permanent variant; for the short term, the temporary-housing strategy stabilizes registration and lets permanent search happen with real information).

**Capacity-respecting communication continuity**

L.B.'s engagement letter establishes written-first communication as a capacity preference. D-07 honors this through arrival: scheduled async check-ins in week 1 (not sync calls), gradual increase to sync touchpoints as capacity returns. The therapist-continuity plan is in place pre-arrival (video sessions continuing on the Atlanta-side cadence for the first ~12 weeks before considering NL-side therapist referral). The household's home-side support contacts have agreed cadence rather than improvised contact.

**Routine before community**

L.B. and Elie are both at risk of trying to *build community* in week 1 because community-building has been a stated value across the engagement. D-07 explicitly defers this — week 1 establishes sleep/eat/walk routine; days 8–30 add functional rhythms; community-building starts days 31+ when the stabilization stack is holding. This is harder for L.B. specifically because community has been the *reason* for many of the move's design choices; the deferral is named in the walkthrough.

### M.K. simulated stress test (US → DE, Blue Card)

**Anmeldung in week 2, not week 1**

Berlin Anmeldung must happen within 14 days of move-in. Drafting week-1 to include Anmeldung is tempting (clear the gate fast) but high-risk for M.K.'s capacity profile — jetlag + sensory load + appointment stress = exactly the conditions for a missed appointment. D-07 schedules Anmeldung for early week 2 (within the legal window) with week 1 reserved for rest, registration-eligible housing confirmation, and initial neighborhood orientation.

**Single GP appointment in days 8–30; specialists deferred**

M.K.'s healthcare profile includes ongoing therapy continuity. D-07 schedules a single GP-registration appointment in the days 8–30 window for basic enrollment and prescription transfer. Specialist consultations (any beyond the GP referral) defer to days 31–60. The therapy continuity is maintained through the Atlanta-side therapist with a planned review at day 60 for whether a Berlin-side referral makes sense.

**No language class in first 30 days**

M.K. has B1/B2 German via Sabine; the temptation to *level up* by enrolling in classes during the first 30 days is real. D-07 explicitly defers: continued Sabine tutoring (which is established and capacity-known) continues; new structured classes don't begin until days 31+. The decision is framed as *protecting the foundation that's already working* rather than *delaying improvement*.

### Counter-example: the optimizing first month

A first-30-days plan that includes: Anmeldung, GP, specialist consultation, dentist, optometrist, gym membership, language class enrollment, two community events, three apartment viewings, financial planner consultation, tax advisor follow-up, and a weekend trip to "explore the surrounding region." Every item is reasonable. The combination is the failure pattern §5 names. The methodology rejects this plan not item-by-item but as a *shape* — too many optimization items concurrent with stabilization items, in a window where capacity is at its post-move minimum.

## 9. Adjacent vocabulary

- **Stabilization**: presence and access in the load-bearing systems. The first-30-days target.
- **Optimization**: refinement on top of stable basics. The days 30–90 and beyond target.
- **Stabilization stack**: the six-item ordered list of load-bearing systems (housing/address → registration → healthcare → banking → comms → capacity protection).
- **Optimization pile**: the set of refinement work that belongs after stabilization.
- **Bridge**: a temporary continuation of a system pre-arrival (insurance bridge, therapy bridge) that ends when the destination-side equivalent stabilizes.
- **Forward motion**: the felt sense of doing something planning-shaped; misleadingly attractive in week 1 because planning has been the dominant mode.
- **Crash pattern**: the failure mode stabilize-before-optimize prevents; specific to client capacity profiles.
- **Review window**: the planned day-90 retrospective; *not* the deadline by which everything must be done.

Stabilize-before-optimize is not the same as:

- **Minimalism**: the methodology doesn't argue for less; it argues for *sequenced more*.
- **Slowness**: stabilization can be intense; the principle is about *order*, not *pace*.
- **Settling**: the temporary apartment is not the permanent apartment, and the methodology never pretends it is.
- **Avoiding optimization**: optimization is the point of moving; the methodology times it correctly.

## 10. How to use this in practice

**During D-07 drafting**: structure the four phases (week 1; days 8–30; days 31–60; days 61–90) explicitly. Place every checklist item in the phase its content earns. If the practitioner finds themselves placing optimization items in week 1 or days 8–30, the placement is the failure mode — relocate the item.

**During capacity-overlay rendering**: D-07's capacity profile should make the stabilization-window's lower capacity *visible*, so the deferral of optimization items is legible as a *response to capacity*, not as artificial restraint.

**During the walkthrough call**: walk the client through the stabilization stack first, then through the optimization pile, naming the deferral explicitly. Clients often experience the deferral as *not enough* until they see what week 1 actually contains; the walkthrough's job is to make the deferred items feel *deferred*, not *missed*.

**During mid-engagement re-evaluation**: if the client has started executing into the optimization pile during what should be stabilization, the response is *not* judgment — it's recalibration. Often the client has unconsciously absorbed the forward-motion reflex (§5.2) and is performing competence; the methodology's response is to re-name the stabilization items still in flight and re-allocate the optimization items to days 31+ deliberately.

**During provider handoffs**: providers engaged during the stabilization window should be told they're operating *during a stabilization window*. This affects communication cadence (slower, more written), expected client decision speed (slower, with more reflection), and recovery-window protection (firm). A provider operating at standard pace can accidentally pressure the client past capacity; explicit framing of the window protects both.

**During practitioner self-check**: the practitioner's own engagement during the client's first 30 days should respect the stabilization frame. Don't schedule mid-month sync check-ins that pull energy away from stabilization work. Don't introduce optimization-pile thinking during the walkthrough of week 1's plan. Practice what the methodology preaches.

**Across engagements**: track which stabilization-stack items most often slip and which optimization-pile items most often get attempted early. Pattern recognition over time sharpens both D-07 drafting and walkthrough framing.

## 11. Open questions (v0.1)

- **Stabilization-stack ordering by destination**: the six-item stack assumes destinations where the registration → banking → healthcare cascade is the operative gating structure. Other destinations may have different gating (e.g., destinations where healthcare enrollment requires employment confirmation requires work permit requires registration). The ordering principle holds; the specific stack adapts. Worth a destination-specific overlay in a future version.
- **Bridge-duration limits**: temporary housing in week 1 might extend 6–8 weeks before permanent housing search begins; insurance bridges might run 30–90 days. The methodology asserts bridges should be *planned* — but how long is "too long" for a bridge that's actually become the permanent solution? Calibrate against the first engagement that surfaces the question.
- **What about identity-specific community as stabilization, not optimization?** Some clients (particularly queer-marginalized, ND, chronic-illness) experience identity-specific community connection as *load-bearing for capacity protection*, not as optimization. The methodology's working answer: explicit identity-specific support contacts established pre-arrival (one or two named people, not "find the community") count as stabilization; broader community-building remains optimization. The line is fuzzy; refine with real engagements.
- **Children and dependents**: a client moving with school-age children faces a school-enrollment gate that's stabilization-shaped (legal requirement, deadline-driven) but lives at the optimization-stack's intersection. D-07 stub schema (per `control-surface-architecture_v2.md`) needs explicit handling. Defer to first engagement with school-age children.
- **Pets**: pet-stabilization (vet registration, food source, anxiety-recovery from transit) is genuine stabilization-stack work but doesn't fit cleanly in the six-item list. May need a pet-overlay similar to capacity overlay.
- **Whether to enforce the rules vs. surface them as guidance**: the studio's Tier 3 validation could flag D-07 phases that violate the rules (optimization items in week 1, etc.). The risk is over-rigidity; the benefit is catching the failure mode before delivery. Lean: surface as warnings; let practitioner override with documented rationale.
- **Client psychology around forward motion**: §5.2's forward-motion reflex is a real pattern but isn't universal. Some clients arrive depleted and over-correct toward slowness, missing time-critical stabilization items. The methodology should hold both possibilities. May warrant a sub-discipline for identifying which pattern a given client is exhibiting; defer to real engagements.
