# Hekswerk Methodology — Table of Contents

**Library version: 0.1 (May 2026)**

This index lists the methodology library's essays grouped thematically. Each essay is identified by its code (M-XX), title, current status, and a one-line description. Essays at v0.1 status are linked and readable; scaffolded essays are listed for orientation but not yet available.

## Status legend

- **v0.1** — Drafted at first-engagement quality. Linked and readable.
- **Scaffold** — Titled and outlined; contents in active development.

## I. Foundations — What things are

These essays define what counts as what in the methodology — what's a system, what's a leverage point, what counts as a connection.

- **[M-01 What Counts as a System](essays/m01-what-counts-as-a-system.md)** · v0.1
  Working definition and six-criterion checklist for identifying systems on the Relocation Systems Map (D-01).

- **[M-04 Leverage Points](essays/m04-leverage-points.md)** · v0.1
  Six-criterion checklist for identifying the three to five decisions whose outcomes change the shape of multiple downstream systems.

- **M-05 Complement Test Beyond Systems** · Scaffold
  Extending M-01's complement test ("is there a professional specialist around this?") to other methodology categories — dependencies, risks, leverage points.

- **M-09 When to Draw an Arrow** · Scaffold
  Discipline for the Dependency Map (D-03) — when a connection deserves a directional arrow, when it remains a soft association, when an apparent loop signals a language problem rather than a real dependency.

## II. Epistemics — Where claims come from

These essays govern how the methodology handles knowledge: what counts as a statement, an inference, an assumption, a confirmation; how confident any claim should be; what counts as an authoritative source; and how the practitioner reads what isn't explicitly stated.

- **[M-02 Reading the Room](essays/m02-reading-the-room.md)** · v0.1
  Discipline of attending to signals the structured questions don't produce — energy shifts, contradictions, performance, capacity strain — during the Deep-Dive Interview. Six-signal taxonomy with response strategies for each.

- **[M-03 Inference vs Statement](essays/m03-inference-versus-statement.md)** · v0.1
  Four-way distinction between statement, inference, assumption, and confirmation. Provenance discipline for client-facing artifacts.

- **[M-06 Confidence Labels](essays/m06-confidence-labels.md)** · v0.1
  Five-label system for marking the basis on which a date or quantity should be trusted: confirmed, provider estimate, planning assumption, client preference, monitor/unknown.

- **[M-13 Verify with Authority](essays/m13-verify-with-authority.md)** · v0.1
  Three-authority framework for verifying load-bearing procedural claims: government office, licensed provider, receiving institution. Includes an explicit list of what does NOT count as authority.

## III. Patterns — Recognizing and resolving

These essays name patterns that recur across engagements and the resolutions that work for them.

- **[M-08 Reusable Circular Dependencies](essays/m08-reusable-circular-dependencies.md)** · v0.1
  Five reusable patterns for resolving apparent circular dependencies in the Dependency Map (D-03), preceded by four diagnostic questions to verify the loop is real before reaching for a pattern.

- **M-16 Stabilize Before Optimize** · Scaffold
  Sequencing principle for the First 90 Days landing plan (D-07) and for engagement pacing more broadly — get something working before refining it.

## IV. Time, capacity, and aging

These essays address how the methodology accounts for time: how claims age, how plans degrade, how capacity is consumed, how arrival itself creates a cliff.

- **M-07 Capacity as Critical Strength / Sequencing Constraint** · Scaffold
  Extending the capacity framing into the Critical Path Timeline (D-02) — how capacity windows determine what can be sequenced when.

- **[M-11 Provider Research Aging](essays/m11-provider-research-aging.md)** · v0.1
  Discipline for the temporal decay of provider information in the Provider Shortlist (D-04). Aging-speed categories per provider type, freshness labels at delivery, refresh triggers. Composes with M-06 confidence labels and M-13 authority verification.

- **[M-15 Capacity as a Non-Pathologizing Risk Category](essays/m15-capacity-as-a-non-pathologizing-risk-category.md)** · v0.1
  Three constraints every capacity row in the Budget & Risk Register (D-06) must satisfy. The non-pathologizing rule: failure must be located in the plan's design, not in the client.

- **M-17 Arrival Cliff** · Scaffold
  The post-arrival drop in structure, support, and context that the engagement must plan around. Informs the First 90 Days landing plan (D-07).

---

## V. Practitioner discipline

These essays name disciplines for how the practitioner operates — not what they produce, but how they show up and what limits they hold.

- **M-10 Endorsement Creep** · Scaffold
  How practitioner statements can drift from "you might want to consider" into "I think you should" into "I recommend X." The discipline that prevents the methodology from quietly becoming directive.

- **M-12 Privacy Posture** · Scaffold
  Why privacy-first architecture (local tooling, encrypted workstation, no cloud AI for client data) is a methodology choice and not just an operational one.

- **M-14 Risk Theater vs Risk Register** · Scaffold
  How risk registers can become theater — visible risk-tracking without actual action. The discipline that keeps the register operational rather than performative.

## Notes

The thematic groupings above are presentational, not architectural — many essays touch more than one cluster, and cross-references between essays often run between clusters.

Where an essay is referenced from another but not yet drafted, the referencing essay marks the reference as forthcoming. Once the scaffolded essay reaches v0.1, the referencing essay's forthcoming-note is removed in its next version-bump.
